What ANKA Is

"ANKA is the execution layer that does the revenue cycle work your team can't get to — and guarantees the results in the contract."
"Execution Layer"
ANKA is not a dashboard, not a staffing company, not a point solution. It is the layer that actually does the work — end to end, without requiring human hand-offs.

Not replacing your team. Filling the structural execution gap. At 3 hrs/appeal, 1 FTE handles ~53 appeals/month. You generate 500–1,000. The math doesn't work — ANKA closes the gap.

3 Levels of AI in RCM
Level 1 — Reports
Surfaces data. Requires humans to act on every insight. Example: "Your denial rate is 12%." Staff still has to work every denial.

Who: Most analytics dashboards, EHR reporting modules.

The tell: You still need a full billing team to act on every report.
Level 2 — Alerts
Flags problems. Still requires humans to act. Example: "High-risk denial detected. Assign to billing team." Staff still writes and submits the appeal.

Who: Most AI point solutions, outsourcers with AI overlays.

The tell: The AI identifies the work but hands it back to your staff.
Level 3 — Execution ★ ANKA
Reads, decides, acts, and tracks — without requiring human intervention. Example: Reads EOB → identifies denial reason → writes payer-specific appeal → submits via portal → tracks outcome.

Who: ANKA. Very few credible competitors at this level in mid-market.

The tell: The AI does the work. Your team's capacity is freed for other priorities.

💢 Pain Points
The 5 core execution gaps that ANKA closes. Know each one deeply — with numbers, mechanisms, and a conversation opener.
Pain Point #1 — Denial Management: The Execution Math
THE EXECUTION MATH — MEMORIZE THIS
1 FTE × 3 hours per appeal = ~53 appeals/month
Mid-market organization generates: 500–1,000 appeals/month
Result: Permanent revenue loss on 35–60% of denials. This is a math problem, not a people problem.
Root Causes
No dedicated denial staff (1–3 people handling everything) · No systematic root cause tracking · Appeal deadlines (90 days) pass while claims sit in queue · ~90% of denials are preventable but no prevention infrastructure exists
ANKA's Response
Reads EOB → identifies denial reason → auto-drafts payer-specific appeal → submits electronically via payer portal → tracks response timeline → auto-follows-up if no response. Zero hand-offs. All logged.
DENIAL MANAGEMENT OPENER Cold Call
"At 3 hours per appeal — which is industry standard — how many of your monthly denials does your team actually work through?" [pause] "Most mid-market groups generate 500 to 1,000 appealable denials a month. At that rate, 1 FTE gets to about 53. The rest age out. That's not a staffing problem — that's a math problem. That's exactly what ANKA was built to close."

Why Underpayments Are Different
Underpayments arrive as payments and LOOK normal. There is no alert. No flag. No one notices. Unlike denials, there's no obvious trigger to review. Most EHRs post payments but do NOT verify against contracted rates — the problem is structural. ANKA automatically reconciles every payment against your contracted rates and flags every underpayment for recovery.
UNDERPAYMENT OPENER Net Collection Rate

[almost always 'no'] "That's the most common answer — and it's where 3 to 5 percent of net revenue quietly disappears every year. On a $20M group, that's $600K to $1M — arriving as payments that look correct, but aren't. ANKA reconciles every payment automatically. Most groups we talk to find underpayments within the first 30 days."
Pain Point #4 — Payer Follow-Up Drain
50% of payer follow-up time is spent waiting, not working. IVR navigation burns 10–15 minutes before reaching a human. Many payers still lack real-time electronic claim status. Infrastructure asymmetry: providers need answers; payer call centers can't keep up. ANKA tracks payer response timelines and auto-follows-up — no staff time wasted waiting on hold.
FOLLOW-UP DRAIN OPENER
"How much of your billing team's day is spent just following up with payers — on hold, navigating IVR, waiting for status updates?" [pause] "Most billing staff tell us it's over 30 percent of their time — just waiting. ANKA tracks every payer response timeline automatically and follows up without any staff time. Your team gets that capacity back to work new claims."
Pain Point #5 — Rural & Mid-Market Amplifiers
Every pain point is worse for ANKA's target market because: No dedicated RCM staff (1–3 people handling everything) · No IT infrastructure for enterprise AI solutions · High turnover — institutional knowledge constantly lost · Can't afford enterprise solutions, too complex for basic tools · Dashboard fatigue: teams stop looking at reports that generate work they can't complete.

ANKA's advantage: Built specifically for this segment. Light implementation, fast results, outcome-based contract so it pays for itself.

Market Categories — Where ANKA Plays
Traditional Outsourcers (Staffing-Based)
Sell labor — onshore, nearshore, offshore. Now claim AI. Reality: task-level augmentation, not execution. Core revenue scales with headcount. The "AI" is a layer on top of the same manual staff model.
AI Point Solutions (Narrow Specialists)
Address specific RCM functions. Mid-market ends up with 2–4 vendors that don't talk to each other. None own the full execution chain. Fragmentation creates its own execution gap.
AI-Washed Vendors (Dashboard + Alerts)
Claim "90% automation" but mean "90% of charts touched by AI." Still require the same manual staff to act on every insight. Pricing scales with volume — the tell. No true execution.
AI-Native Execution ★ ANKA's Category
End-to-end execution without human hand-offs. No credible player owns this space in mid-market at scale. Smarter Technologies targets enterprise only. This is the white space ANKA owns.
